### Title

### Body

20 years ago, I graduated with a Computer Science degree, never imagining I'd witness a technological revolution.

From writing COBOL for banking systems, to building 10M+ user travel platforms, to 8 years at VMware (pre-sales → delivery → R&D), and now serving as AI Expert at Lenovo—I've seen infrastructure evolve from "supporting business" to "driving innovation."

**Why This Book?**

In the past year, I've talked to 100+ CTOs. They all share similar pain points:

❌ "Bought GPUs, but utilization is under 30%"
❌ "Training jobs hit network bottlenecks halfway"
❌ "Electricity bills are terrifying—boss asks about ROI"
❌ "So many MLOps tools, but team doesn't know how to use them"

AI infrastructure isn't just "buy hardware + set up environment." It's an engineering discipline requiring systems thinking.

📚 12 chapters, 150,000 words, covering:
